Llama Diet Information

Llamas are herbivores, which means they only eat plants. They primarily graze on grasses, but they also eat a variety of other plants, including shrubs, lichens, and even some fungi. In their native habitats in South America, they have adapted to the sparse vegetation found in the Andes mountains.

In captivity, llamas are often fed a diet of hay and grass. Some owners also provide their llamas with specially formulated llama pellets, which contain a balance of nutrients to ensure they stay healthy. Fresh vegetables and fruits can also be given as treats, but should not make up the bulk of their diet.
