Anatomy and Biology of Llamas

  1. Height / Weight / General Size Information

    2. Llamas are typically,

    • Height: Adult llamas stand approximately 5.5 to 6 feet tall at the shoulder.
    • Weight: They weigh between 280 to 450 pounds, with variations based on gender and environmental factors.
    • General Size: Llamas have a slender, graceful build with long legs and necks adapted for their mountainous habitat.

  2. Significant or Unique Physical Characteristics

    3. Llamas have several distinctive physical traits,

    • Coat: They have a thick, double-layered coat consisting of coarse guard hairs and a soft undercoat that provides insulation against cold temperatures.
    • Ears: Llamas have long, banana-shaped ears that can swivel independently to detect sounds from different directions.
    • Teeth: They have a dental pad (no upper incisors) and teeth adapted for grazing on tough vegetation.
    • Camelid Features: Llamas are part of the camelid family, sharing traits such as padded feet and a split upper lip.
