Llama Habitat Information

Llamas are native to the high altitudes of the Andes mountains in South America. They have adapted to the rugged terrain and can be found at elevations ranging from 7,500 to 13,000 feet above sea level. These regions are characterized by rocky landscapes, sparse vegetation, and extreme weather conditions.

In their natural habitat, llamas graze on the native grasses and plants that grow in the Andean highlands. They are well-suited to the cold, dry climate and can survive on the limited resources available in these harsh environments. Their thick wool coats provide insulation against the cold, and their padded feet help them navigate the rocky terrain.
